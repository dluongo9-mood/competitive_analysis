⚠️
Regulatory Alert — Market Restructuring Ahead 0.4mg THC cap effective Nov 12, 2026 — click to expand
The FY2026 Agriculture Appropriations Act (signed Nov 2025) redefines hemp to include total THC and caps finished products at 0.4mg THC per container, effective Nov 12, 2026. This effectively bans most hemp-derived THC products currently on the market. The alternative CSRA bill proposes a more permissive framework (5mg/serving, 50mg/container). Current market data represents a snapshot of a market that may fundamentally change within months.
If 0.4mg rule takes effect: The hemp-derived THC gummy channel ($1.3B–$2.7B) effectively goes to zero in current form. Some revenue migrates to licensed dispensary (states with legal rec), some reformulates to CBD/minor cannabinoids, and some shifts to the supplement gummy market. Net TAM could shrink to ~$3.1B (dispensary + Amazon only).
If CSRA passes instead: 5mg/serving, 50mg/container preserves most current products. TAM largely intact with modest reformulation.

Broader Context: Supplement Gummies Compete for the Same Consumer
The US supplement gummy market (melatonin, ashwagandha, nootropics) is $4.2B–$5.5B (2024, GM Insights / Fortune Business Insights), growing at 10–13% CAGR. Supplement gummies sell at Walmart, Target, CVS, and Amazon; their market size is based on retail scanner data (IRI/SPINS/Nielsen). Our cannabis/hemp TAM above is a modeled estimate combining dispensary POS data with Brightfield survey-based sizing — less precise. The two numbers are not directly comparable, but both markets target the same functional needs.

Model Formula
Est. Gummy Revenue = Monthly Visits × Gummy Traffic % × Conv. Rate × AOV
Conservative Model
• Gummy % = catalog share (% of brand's SKUs that are gummies)
• Conv = max(1%, 3% − bounce × 2%)  range: 1–3%
• AOV = median gummy price × 1.3 cart factor
Calibrated Model
• Gummy % = catalog share × 1.8 (gummies drive outsized traffic)
• Conv = max(1%, 5% − bounce × 4%)  range: 1–5%
• AOV = median gummy price × 2.5 cart factor (bundles, subscribe & save)
Brightfield (survey)
Company market share × $5.42B total hemp THC market ÷ 12. All products (not just gummies). Survey-based — directional, not transactional.

Data Sources
• Monthly visits: Zyla Site Traffic API (SimilarWeb data), Feb 2026 snapshot

• Median gummy price: From scraped product data, variant-level pricing
• Bounce rate: Zyla/SimilarWeb engagement metrics

Limitations
• Traffic data is a single Feb 2026 snapshot — seasonal variation not captured
• Gummy catalog % assumes catalog share approximates revenue share; in practice, hero products (gummies) often drive outsized revenue vs catalog weight
• Conversion rate and AOV are modeled, not observed — actual values vary by brand maturity, marketing spend, and purchase funnel
• Model captures DTC website revenue only — excludes wholesale, retail, marketplace (Amazon/Faire), and dispensary channels
• Calibrated against 3 brands; accuracy for brands with very different profiles (e.g., beverage-first, dispensary-first) may differ
